Gentle, sociable, aquatic.

Anything intresting about Capybara

World's Largest Rodent

Giant Gentle Giants

Capybaras hold the title of being the largest rodents in the world. They can weigh up to 140 pounds (about 64 kilograms) and grow to be over 4 feet <1.2 meters > in length. Despite their impressive size, these creatures are known for their gentle and social nature, making them unique and fascinating members of the animal kingdom.

Semi-Aquatic Lifestyle

Masters of Land and Water

Capybaras are excellent swimmers and spend a significant amount of time in the water. With their webbed feet, they are well-adapted to their aquatic habitats . They can stay submerged for up to five minutes, a handy skill that helps them evade predators and keep cool in hot weather, showcasing their remarkable adaptability.

Social Animals

The Communicative Creatures

Highly social, capybaras live in groups that can range from 10 to 20 individuals, and sometimes even up to 100. They communicate using a variety of sounds, including purring, barking, and whistling, making them quite vocal. This complex social structure and communication system highlight the pre and cooperative nature of capybaras.

Types of Capybaras

  • Common Capybara (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris)
  • Lesser Capybara (Hydrochoerus isthmius)
  • Greater Capybara (Hydrochoerus maximus)
  • Mountain Capybara (Hydrochoerus sometiemensis)
  • Forest Capybara (Hydrochoerus sylvestris)
